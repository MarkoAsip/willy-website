why willy

Willy was born out of a simple personal need.

I love deep breathing. I know what it does for the body and mind. But somewhere between meetings, emails, and the general noise of the day — I kept forgetting to do it.

So I built a reminder. Something gentle, non-intrusive, that would nudge me a few times a day to just stop and take one conscious breath.

While exploring different breathing techniques, I kept coming back to one — the belly-first, chest-expanding method that activates the vagus nerve. Of everything I tried, nothing worked as quickly or as reliably for an on-the-go reset. You feel the difference immediately.

That became the Willy Technique.

Willy is not a wellness platform. It's not trying to change your life. It's just a quiet companion — reminding you, a few times a day, to come back to yourself.

One conscious breath at a time.

the willy technique

Four phases, one breath

Belly out

Breathe in through your nose. Let your belly fill first.

Chest up

Keep breathing in. Let your chest rise.

Hold and feel

Pause. Hold. Notice the relaxation.

Release

Breathe out slowly through your mouth. Chest first, then belly.

what it does

Small moments, regularly

what willy intentionally does not have

No streaks. No accounts. No noise.

Technology should not always stimulate — sometimes it should regulate. Willy is designed to feel calm, quiet, warm, almost invisible. It should never feel addictive, overwhelming, gamified, or productivity‑driven.

  • No accounts or profiles
  • No streaks or achievements
  • No statistics dashboards
  • No social features
  • No breathing programs or AI coaching
  • No gamification
  • No aggressive notifications

how it works

Set it once, forget it

Choose a reminder interval — every 30, 60, or 90 minutes — and set your active hours. Willy sends a gentle, vibration‑only notification at each interval. Take one conscious breath, and continue your day. Everything runs locally on your phone, offline, with nothing to set up beyond that.
